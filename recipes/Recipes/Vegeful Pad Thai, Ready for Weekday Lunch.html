Vegeful Pad Thai, Ready for Weekday Lunch

Servings: Servings: 6 with some extra leftover Source: reddit.com

Ingredients

600g chicken thigh, boneless skinless, sliced into thin strips

3 Capsicums, sliced into thin pieces (I used green, yellow and red for colour but feel free to use a sweetish Veg of your preference such as green beans)

2 med sized carrots, sliced thinly

1 bag bean sprouts (optional so add as much as you want)

3 spring onions, chopped roughly

2 cloves garlic, minced

Sprigs of coriander and lemon or lime for garnish

4 eggs, lightly beaten

Sauce:

2 tbsp rice wine vinegar

1 tbsp fish sauce or to taste

Chilli flakes

3 tbsp brown sugar

Directions

Prep all ingredients as per above and have them ready. Don't start soaking the noodles till they are ready to be added to the stir fry.

Fry chicken in oil using wok or large non stick pan on med high heat till cooked through, add a dash of salt. Roughly 3 to 5 Min.

Make a well in centre of chicken and pour in egg. Wait 1 min before stirring the egg to get some texture in the egg. Stir egg into chicken until cooked and set chicken and egg aside in separate bowl.

Using the same pan, cook the capsicum, carrot on med high heat until almost cooked through (5min ish) add bean sprouts to mix if using and cook another 2 Min. Add a dash of salt and set aside.

I assembled this in 2 batches because it wouldn't all fit into my wok. This is a good idea if you want everything to mix evenly. Hence, I soaked half the noodles at a time.

Soak half your noodles in just boiled water.

In the meantime, cook half the garlic and spring onions. Portion off half the chicken, egg, veg and add to pan to reheat.

Add half your sauce mix and stir through.

Add noodles once drained and oil to prevent too much sticking.

Taste to see if additional seasoning is needed. Eg if bland add more fish sauce. Or rice vinegar if not sour enough. Add chilli flakes for desired levels of spice.

Repeat the steps above with the other half of your soaked noodles.

Portion into containers and sprinkle over the coriander and add lemon or lime wedge.

If freezing, omit the lemon.
